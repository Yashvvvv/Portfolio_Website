# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and freelance clients who receive a portfolio link. Two modes: a technical hiring manager scanning for engineering depth and range; a potential client assessing trust and capability before a project conversation. Both need to leave with confidence, not entertainment.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Yash Sharma, a full-stack engineer specializing in React, Spring Boot, Kotlin, and AI/LLM integration. The portfolio communicates depth of skill, breadth of stack, and a focused expertise in AI-native development. Success: a visitor knows what Yash builds, trusts the quality, and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Sharp. Precise. Quietly confident.

Not loud about it — the work speaks. Not cold — there is genuine curiosity and ambition visible in the AI focus. The voice is that of an engineer who has shipped things, not a student showing off.

## Anti-references

- Generic developer portfolio templates (same hero/cards/timeline structure as ten thousand others)
- Gamer/neon aesthetic (RGB highlights, controller SVGs, particle soup, neon glows)
- Corporate/stiff (big company landing page, overly formal, cold)
- Over-decorated motion (floating orbs for decoration, entrance animations on every paragraph)

## Design Principles

1. **Technical credibility through specificity.** Show real code, real problems, real stacks. No emoji standing in for content.
2. **Restraint as authority.** Every decorative element removed raises signal-to-noise ratio. If it does not communicate, it does not ship.
3. **Precision in every detail.** Type scale, spacing rhythm, color values — each decision is intentional, not a default.
4. **Motion earns its place.** Entrance animations exist to direct attention, not to demonstrate capability.
5. **Dual-audience legibility.** A recruiter and a client read differently; the information architecture serves both without compromising for either.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All body text 4.5:1 contrast on background. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Keyboard navigable.
