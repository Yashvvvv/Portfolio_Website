I want to scaffold a production‑ready, dynamic portfolio website (Next.js or React) with a dark‑purple‑toned theme (accented by white, subtle textures, and occasional black highlights) that tells my story as a fast‑learning, problem‑solving full‑stack explorer. It must be clean, modular, responsive, and easy to maintain. 

### 1. Overall Design
- **Color Palette:** Deep purple background (#2B0A3D or similar), white text, subtle textured overlays (carbon‑fiber or grain). Buttons and highlights in lavender/pink‑purple gradients. Occasional black cards for contrast.
- **Layout Style:** Card‑based sections with generous margins/padding. Each “card” floats on the page with a slight drop shadow and 2xl rounded corners. Main container background is purple; card backgrounds alternate between white (with purple text/buttons) and black (with white/purple text).
- **Animations:** Use Framer Motion for subtle hover lift on cards, fade‑in sections, and tab transitions.

### 2. Components & Sections
1. **Hero Section**
   - Full‑width purple background with moving gradient or animated particle texture.
   - Headline: “Yash Sharma | Full‑Stack Explorer & Problem Solver”
   - Subline: “From Kotlin Android prototypes to React & Spring Boot web solutions.”
   - Call‑to‑Action button: “Explore My Journey” (scrolls to timeline).

2. **Interactive Timeline / Tech Map**
   - Horizontal or vertical scroll. Each node: an icon (Android, React, Spring, AI, Prompt‑Engineering).
   - Hover or click expands node into a mini‑card: date, tech learned, one‑line “aha” moment.
   - Visually highlight your prompt‑engineering & Gen‑AI tools (ComfyUI, Stable Diffusion, etc.).

3. **About Me Narrative Card**
   - Alternating card (white bg): Two paragraphs:
     - **Journey Start:** Kotlin/Android basics and early prototyping.
     - **Pivot & Growth:** How React + Spring Boot became my main stack—driven by product‑first thinking and college‑job necessity.
   - Include a small geeky badge section (“Prompt‑Engineer,” “Gen‑AI Enthusiast,” “Hackathon Addict”).

4. **Projects Section with Dynamic Tabs**
   - **Tabs:** “Android Projects” and “Web Projects” at the top of this card.
   - On click, swap content without page reload (animated underline).  
   - **Project Cards:** For each, show:
     - Screenshot/GIF placeholder
     - Title & date
     - Problem statement
     - Tech used (with little icons)
     - Live link or “Coming Soon” badge
   - Cards arranged in a grid with consistent margins/padding.

5. **Skills & Tech Graph Card**
   - Filterable tag cloud or icon grid.
   - Categories: Mobile, Web, AI & LLMs, DevOps.
   - Click a tag to highlight related case studies above.

6. **Call‑to‑Action Footer**
   - Dark card (black bg) with white text: “Ready to solve your toughest problem?”  
   - Buttons: “Let’s Talk” (mailto:) / “Download Résumé”

7. **Global Elements**
   - **Navbar:** Sticky, transparent on top, switches to purple‑black on scroll. Links to each section.
   - **Theme Toggle:** Light mode (white bg cards on purple) vs. Dark mode (all black/purple cards).
   - **README.md:** Instructions on replacing placeholder data (text, images, links), color variables (Tailwind config), and deploying on Replit.

### 3. Tech Stack & Boilerplate
- **Framework:** Next.js (create‑next‑app) with Tailwind CSS and Framer Motion.
- **State Management:** React Context or simple useState for tabs/timeline.
- **Styling:** Tailwind utility classes; configure a custom purple theme in `tailwind.config.js`.
- **Deployment:** One‑click Replit deploy script in `package.json`.
